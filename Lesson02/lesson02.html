Design Principles

White Space and Clean Design

White space is important if you want to make a website look great. White space is unsed space in a website. With white space, you improve the readability of your webage because you give each element breathing space so that it is easier for the eyes to read them. Apple's webpage is a perfect example of using white space to add emphasis on their product they are selling.

Contrast

Contrast is used in web design to places emphasis on a element on a webpage. You can place contrast by using contrasting colors such as white or a lighter text on a black or darker background. You can use contrast with putting a smaller object next to a larger object or using a differnt font style next to a different but complemtary font style. One good example of contrast is Crumbl Cookies webpage. They use contrast to create a hierachy between the different elements. They made the video of the cookies they sell the biggest so that they can draw the user to crave the cookie and immdiatly turn to the black button with white text that says order now.

Hick's Law

Hick's Law states that the more choices that is offered to a user then they will spend more time trying to make a choice. You want to make your users life easier by making your webpage easy to navigate through by reducing the number of options and making your homepage simple. For example with Thursday Boots the page is simplified by the first two buttons you see is whether you are shopping for men's boots or women's boots. Thursday Boots' don't throw every picture of every product they sell out of the gate but instead just lead users to filter down their choice of boots they are looking for them making their shopping experience go smooth for them.
